# Scientific Framework: KeepWell AI

This document outlines the evidence-based theories that inform KeepWell AI's design decisions. Every nudge, timing choice, and interaction pattern is grounded in peer-reviewed behavioral science.

---

## 1. Holistic Framework: SAMHSA 8 Dimensions of Wellness

**Source:** Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), 2016

Wellness is not the absence of disease. It is an active, ongoing process of making choices toward a healthy and fulfilling life across eight interconnected dimensions:

| Dimension | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| Physical | Recognizing the need for physical activity, healthy foods, and sleep |
| Emotional | Coping effectively with life and creating satisfying relationships |
| Occupational | Personal satisfaction and enrichment from one's work |
| Social | Developing a sense of connection, belonging, and support system |
| Intellectual | Recognizing creative abilities and expanding knowledge and skills |
| Environmental | Good health by occupying pleasant, stimulating environments |
| Financial | Satisfaction with current and future financial situations |
| Spiritual | Expanding a sense of purpose and meaning in life |

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Tips rotate across all 8 dimensions to prevent tunnel vision on just physical health (the most common wellness app trap).

## 2. Timing: Ultradian Rhythms

**Source:** Rossi, E. L. (1991). *The 20-Minute Break*. Tarcher.

Human cognitive performance operates in 90-120 minute cycles (Basic Rest-Activity Cycle). After each cycle, the brain requires 15-20 minutes of lower-intensity activity for memory consolidation and neural recovery.

**Key findings:**
- Sustained focus beyond 90 minutes leads to diminishing returns
- Brief breaks (even 5 min) during the trough restore attention
- Ignoring the trough leads to compensatory stress hormones (cortisol spike)

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Break reminders are timed at 90-minute intervals, not arbitrary clock hours.

---

## 3. Timing: Circadian Rhythm & Chronobiology

**Source:** Walker, M. (2017). *Why We Sleep*. Scribner; Roenneberg, T. (2012). *Internal Time*. Harvard UP.

The body's internal clock governs alertness, mood, and cognitive capacity throughout the day:

| Time Window | State | Optimal Activity |
|-------------|-------|-----------------|
| 06:00-09:00 | Cortisol rising | Light planning, routine tasks |
| 09:00-12:00 | Peak alertness | Deep work, complex problems |
| 12:00-14:00 | Post-prandial dip | Social, low-stakes tasks |
| 14:00-16:00 | Second wind | Creative work, brainstorming |
| 16:00-18:00 | Declining focus | Meetings, admin, email |
| 21:00-23:00 | Melatonin onset | Wind-down, reflection |

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Nudge content adapts to time of day. Morning nudges focus on intention-setting; evening nudges focus on reflection and wind-down.

## 4. Behavior Change: Nudge Theory

**Source:** Thaler, R. & Sunstein, C. (2008). *Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness*. Yale UP.

A nudge is any aspect of choice architecture that predictably alters behavior without forbidding options or significantly changing economic incentives. Effective nudges are:

- **Easy** — Default to the healthy choice; reduce friction
- **Attractive** — Frame positively; make the nudge feel rewarding
- **Social** — Leverage social norms ("most people take a break every 90 min")
- **Timely** — Deliver at the moment of decision, not before or after

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Tips are short (under 30 words for the action), framed positively, and delivered at decision-relevant moments.

---

## 5. Motivation: Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

**Source:** Deci, E. L. & Ryan, R. M. (2000). The "what" and "why" of goal pursuits. *Psychological Inquiry*, 11(4), 227-268.

Intrinsic motivation requires three psychological needs:

| Need | Definition | KeepWell Design |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Autonomy | Feeling of choice and volition | User controls dimensions, frequency, language |
| Competence | Feeling effective and capable | Tips include "why it works" so user learns |
| Relatedness | Feeling connected to others | Social dimension nudges; community framing |

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Never commands; always suggests. User can dismiss or customize. Each tip explains the science so the user feels empowered, not nagged.

---

## 6. Behavior Change Stages: Transtheoretical Model (TTM)

**Source:** Prochaska, J. O. & Velicer, W. F. (1997). The transtheoretical model of health behavior change. *American Journal of Health Promotion*, 12(1), 38-48.

People move through stages when adopting new behaviors:

1. **Precontemplation** — Not yet aware; need awareness nudges
2. **Contemplation** — Aware but ambivalent; need pros/cons
3. **Preparation** — Intending to act; need concrete steps
4. **Action** — Actively changing; need reinforcement
5. **Maintenance** — Sustaining change; need habit anchors

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Initial nudges are lighter (awareness). As the user engages, nudges progressively offer more specific action steps.

---

## 7. Emotional Regulation: Affect Labeling

**Source:** Lieberman, M. D. et al. (2007). Putting feelings into words: Affect labeling disrupts amygdala activity. *Psychological Science*, 18(5), 421-428.

Simply naming an emotion ("I feel anxious") reduces amygdala reactivity by up to 43%. This is why journaling and check-ins work even without a therapist.

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Emotional dimension nudges ask users to name their current feeling, not to fix it. The labeling itself is the intervention.

---

## 8. Attention: Attention Restoration Theory (ART)

**Source:** Kaplan, S. (1995). The restorative benefits of nature. *Journal of Environmental Psychology*, 15(3), 169-182.

Directed attention is a finite resource that depletes with sustained use. Natural environments restore attention through "soft fascination" (involuntary, effortless attention to natural stimuli).

**Key findings:**
- Even 40 seconds of looking at a green roof improves sustained attention
- 20 seconds of window-gazing activates parasympathetic nervous system
- Indoor plants reduce stress biomarkers (cortisol) by 12%

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Environmental dimension nudges suggest micro-nature interactions (window gazing, plant care, fresh air) as attention restoration strategies.

---

## 9. Work Stress: Job Demands-Resources Model (JD-R)

**Source:** Bakker, A. B. & Demerouti, E. (2007). The Job Demands-Resources model. *Journal of Managerial Psychology*, 22(3), 309-328.

Employee well-being is a function of the balance between:
- **Job Demands** — workload, time pressure, emotional labor, role conflict
- **Job Resources** — autonomy, feedback, social support, development opportunities

When demands consistently exceed resources, burnout follows. When resources match or exceed demands, engagement increases.

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Occupational dimension nudges help users identify and activate resources (boundaries, delegation, support-seeking) rather than just managing demands.

---

## 10. Sleep Hygiene: Two-Process Model

**Source:** Borbely, A. A. (1982). A two-process model of sleep regulation. *Human Neurobiology*, 1(3), 195-204.

Sleep quality depends on:
- **Process S** (homeostatic sleep drive) — builds with time awake
- **Process C** (circadian alerting signal) — governed by light exposure

Disrupting either process (late blue-light exposure, irregular schedule, caffeine after 2 PM) degrades sleep architecture.

**Application in KeepWell AI:** Evening wind-down nudges reference specific sleep hygiene practices with timing rationale (e.g., "no screens 30 min before bed reduces sleep onset latency by 20 min").
